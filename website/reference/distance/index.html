Distance & Routes

Distance, range, and road squares are three different things. Here's how each works.

Three Different Concepts

Concept What It Counts Used For
Distance Tile border crossings (shortest path) Effective price in competition
Range Tile border crossings (max on route) Employee action reach (Cart Operator = 2, Truck Driver = 5)
Road Squares Individual grid squares along a road Tracing the actual path your collector travels

📌 Key Rule

Always say "tile borders" when explaining distance and range. Never say "spaces" or "squares" for these — reserve those words for the road grid.

How to Trace a Road Route

  1. Start from your restaurant's Entrance Corner (not from anywhere on the restaurant).
  2. Follow connected road squares from that entrance.
  3. Each tile border you cross counts as 1 toward your range.
  4. You can backtrack through previously-visited road squares.
  5. You cannot immediately reverse direction (step back onto the square you just came from).
  6. Each drink source can only be collected from once per employee per route.

Common Pitfalls

⚠️ Bridges Don't Connect

Two roads can cross at a bridge without connecting. A cart on Road A cannot switch to Road B at a bridge. Think of them as overpasses — the roads are on different floors.

⚠️ Entrance Corner = Everything

Your Entrance Corner is the starting point for drink routes, distance calculations, campaign range, and employee action range. A restaurant has only one entrance corner (unless a Drive-In is active). Its position relative to roads determines your entire spatial game.

⚠️ Range ≠ Road Squares

"Range 2" means you can cross 2 tile borders — not move 2 road squares. A single tile might have many road squares. Don't confuse the two.
