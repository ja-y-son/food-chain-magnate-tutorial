Dinnertime Checklist

Step-by-step guide to resolving sales each round.

Sales Resolution Flowchart

Does the house have demand?

No →

Skip this house. No sale.

Yes ↓

Is it connected by road to a restaurant?

No →

No sale. Demand stays (or is wasted, depending on rules).

Yes ↓

Does the restaurant have ALL demanded items?

No →

No sale from this restaurant. Check other connected restaurants.

Yes ↓

Is there one restaurant or multiple that can fulfill?

One →

Sale! That restaurant gets the payment.

Multiple →

Competition! Lowest price wins. Ties broken by shortest distance.

Payment Calculation

📌 Key Rule

Each item sold earns $10 from the bank. Houses with a garden pay double — $20 per item.

🔍 Example: Regular House

A house demands 1 burger + 1 drink. You have both in stock.

  • 2 items × $10 = $20 from the bank

🔍 Example: House with Garden

Same sale to a house with a garden:

  • 2 items × $10 = $20 base
  • Garden doubles it → $40 from the bank

Competition Tiebreakers

When multiple restaurants can fulfill a house's demand, resolve by:

  1. Lowest effective price (item price + distance in tile crossings)
  2. Most Waitresses played this round (tiebreaker)
  3. Earliest in turn order (final tiebreaker)

⚠️ Watch Out

Effective price determines the winner, but you're only paid the item price. Distance penalizes your competitiveness — it doesn't increase your revenue.
