Glossary

Bank Break
The game-ending trigger. When the bank runs out of money during Dinnertime, the game ends immediately. The player with the most cash wins.
Bridge
A map feature where two roads cross without connecting. A cart or truck on one road cannot switch to the other at a bridge. Think of them as overpasses — the roads are on different levels.
Busy Marketeer
A Marketeer who has an active campaign running. Busy Marketeers are locked in your structure — they can't be used, trained, or reassigned until their campaign ends.
Campaign
The act of placing demand counters on houses through marketing. Each marketing employee runs a campaign that targets houses within range of your restaurant.
CEO
Your starting card and the root of your company tree. The CEO provides 3 work slots and 1 hire action per round. The CEO is always in play and cannot be fired.
Cleanup
The final phase of each round. During cleanup, unsold food is discarded, the turn order is adjusted, and players prepare for the next round.
Coming Soon
A placeholder marker on the board indicating that a map tile will be revealed when a player builds a restaurant or places a house nearby. These tiles expand the playable area during the game.
Connected by Road
Two locations are connected by road if you can trace a continuous path of road segments between them. This is required for marketing reach and food delivery.
Demand Counter
A small token placed on a house showing what food or drink that house wants to buy. Demand is created by marketing employees and persists until fulfilled or removed.
Dinnertime
Phase 4 — the phase where food is sold. Houses are checked in numerical order. Each house with demand buys from the qualifying restaurant with the lowest effective price. Payments come from the bank. No player decisions are made during this phase.
Distance
The number of tile border crossings between two points on the map (shortest path). Distance is used to calculate effective price during competition. Being on the same tile = distance 0.
Drive-In
A sign placed by a Local or Regional Manager that gives your restaurant entrances at all 4 corners until the end of the round. This massively expands your reach for drink collection, marketing, and customer access. Removed during Cleanup.
Effective Price
Item price + distance (in tile border crossings). Used during competition when multiple restaurants can serve the same house. The restaurant with the lowest effective price wins the customer. Note: the seller is still only paid the item price, not the effective price.
Entrance Corner
The specific corner of your restaurant tile marked as the entrance. All routes, distances, and ranges are measured from this point. A restaurant has only one entrance corner (unless a Drive-In sign is active, which gives all 4 corners).
Entry-Level Employee
The basic tier of employees available for hire. In the Introductory Game, these include Recruiting Girl, Marketing Trainee, Trainer, Kitchen Trainee, and Cart Operator. They can be promoted to more powerful versions.
Garden
An upgrade placed on a house. Houses with gardens can hold 5 demand counters (instead of 3) and pay double price for everything they buy ($20 per item instead of $10).
Item Price
The base price of each food or drink item, starting at $10. This is what the selling restaurant collects per item from the bank. Can be modified by pricing employees in the full game.
On the Beach
An employee not placed in a work slot for the current round. Benched employees do nothing — no actions, no production. In the full game, they still cost salary. New hires always start On the Beach.
Range
The maximum road distance an employee's effect can reach from your restaurant. Marketing range determines which houses can receive demand; delivery range determines which houses you can sell to.
Restructuring
The phase at the start of each round where you rearrange your organization chart. You can move employees between work slots, put them on the beach, or bring them back from the beach.
Road Route
The specific path along roads connecting your restaurant to a house. The shortest road route determines distance for marketing, delivery, and competitive pricing.
Stock
Food or drink items your kitchen employees have prepared. Stock sits in your restaurant until Dinnertime, when it can be sold to houses with matching demand. Unsold stock is discarded at the end of the round.
Tile Border
The edge where two map tiles meet. Roads that run to a tile border connect to roads on the adjacent tile, forming continuous routes across the board.
Work Slot
A position in your company structure where an employee can be placed to work. Your CEO provides 3 work slots. Some managers provide additional slots. Only employees in work slots perform their actions each round.
