Bank Reserve Cards

The bank's money supply controls when the game ends — and you help decide how much is in it.

How the Bank Works

The bank starts with $50 per player (e.g., $100 for 2 players, $200 for 4 players). Every time a player sells food, the payment comes from the bank. When the bank runs out of money, it "breaks."

📌 Key Rule

The game ends after the bank breaks twice. Not once — twice.

Bank Reserve Cards

During setup, each player secretly chooses one of three bank reserve cards: $100, $200, or $300. These are placed face-down near the bank. The other two cards are returned to the box unseen.

First Bank Break

When the bank runs out of money for the first time:

  1. Finish paying out any remaining sales for the current Dinnertime
  2. All face-down reserve cards are revealed
  3. The bank is refilled with the total amount shown on all reserve cards
  4. Continue playing normally

Second Bank Break

When the bank runs out a second time:

  1. Finish paying out the current Dinnertime
  2. The game ends immediately — skip Payday, Marketing, and Cleanup
  3. The player with the most cash wins

💡 Tip

You don't pay salaries in the final round. No need to fire your staff before the end!

Strategic Choice

Your reserve card choice is a strategic decision about game length:

  • $100 (short game) — favors aggressive early selling. The bank refills with less money, ending the game sooner.
  • $200 (medium) — balanced. Gives time to build an engine while keeping the game reasonable.
  • $300 (long game) — favors heavy investment in hiring and training. More time to build a powerful company before the end.

⚠️ Watch Out

You won't know what your opponents chose until the first break. If they all pick $100 and you pick $300, you might be investing in an engine that never pays off because the game ends too quickly. Flexibility is key.

CEO Slots After the Break

In the full game, the reserve cards you chose also affect the number of CEO work slots available after the first bank break. This is shown on each reserve card — a higher reserve card may give you fewer work slots, representing the trade-off between game length and company flexibility.

First to Have $20 Milestone

The player who earns this milestone can look at all face-down reserve cards at any time. This gives you perfect information about when the game will end — an enormous strategic advantage.
