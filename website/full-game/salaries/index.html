FCM Tutorial

Salaries & Payday

In the full game, employees cost money every round.

How Salaries Work

During Phase 5 (Payday), you must pay a $5 salary for each employee that shows a salary icon. This includes employees:

  • Played in your company structure (work slots)
  • On the Beach (hired but not working)
  • Busy running marketing campaigns

The CEO does not have a salary.

📌 Key Rule

You pay salary for ALL employees — even ones sitting On the Beach doing nothing. Every hire is a commitment.

Salary Discounts

Some sources reduce your total salary bill. All discounts are mandatory — you must apply them even if it's unhelpful (e.g., you can't ignore a discount to qualify for the "$20+ in Salaries" milestone).

SourceDiscount
Unused Recruiting Manager action−$5 per action
Unused HR Director action−$5 per action
"First to Train" milestone−$15 (permanent)
"First Billboard Campaign" milestoneNo salary for any Marketeers

Your total owed can never go below $0.

The Salary Trap

⚠️ Watch Out

New players often hire and train aggressively without considering the ongoing salary cost. A large org chart can drain your cash faster than you earn it. Always check whether your income will cover payroll before expanding.

If you cannot pay all salaries, you must fire salaried employees until you can afford the remainder. In the extreme case (no cash), you must fire all salaried employees.

Firing Employees

Before paying salaries, you may voluntarily fire any employees in your structure or On the Beach. Fired employees return to the general supply.

⚠️ Watch Out

You can never voluntarily fire busy Marketeers. They keep running their campaigns (and costing you salary) until you literally can't afford them and have no one else to fire. Their campaign stays on the board even after they're fired.

Payment Rules Summary

  • Salaries are paid during Phase 5 of every round
  • The CEO has no salary
  • Each salaried employee costs $5
  • Apply all discounts first, then pay the total
  • If you can't afford it, fire employees until you can
  • You never go into debt — $0 is the floor
  • You do not pay salaries in the final round (when the game ends after the second bank break)
