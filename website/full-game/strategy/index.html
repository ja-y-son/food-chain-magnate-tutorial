Strategy Tips

Practical advice for your first full game and beyond.

Early Game (Rounds 1–3)

  1. Don't over-hire early. Every employee adds $5 to your salary. Start lean and expand only when you have income to support it.
  2. Marketing is your engine's ignition. Without campaigns, houses have no demand and no one can sell anything. Getting a Marketeer working early is critical.
  3. Match production to demand. Only produce items that houses actually want. Unsold food is thrown away during Cleanup.
  4. Race for key milestones selectively. Not every milestone is worth pursuing. Pick 2-3 that fit your strategy. "First to Train" ($15 salary reduction) and marketed-item milestones (+$5 per sale) are among the strongest.

Mid Game (Rounds 4–7)

  1. Location matters. Place restaurants near houses with existing demand. Being closer means a lower effective price and fewer competitors.
  2. Watch what your opponents produce. If they're marketing burgers near your houses, decide: compete with lower prices, or market something different to avoid direct competition.
  3. Time your training wisely. Upgraded employees are powerful but costly. Train too early and you waste salary; train too late and you miss opportunities.
  4. Strategic advertising. You can hurt opponents by advertising items they can't produce. But be careful — you might also be helping them sell more later!
  5. Gardens are gold. Houses with gardens pay double for every item. They're lucrative targets, but expect competition for them.

Late Game & Endgame

  1. Watch the bank reserve. The game ends after the bank breaks twice. When reserves get low, shift from building to maximizing sales.
  2. Late-game acceleration. The game often accelerates dramatically near the end. You can win by earning cash only in the last round if you've prepared well — but you could also still be preparing for a round that never comes.
  3. Plan your endgame. As the bank shrinks, make sure you've converted your engine into cash. Having the best employees means nothing if you haven't sold enough food.
  4. House order matters. Houses are resolved in numerical order during Dinnertime. If you serve all your stock to low-numbered houses, you can't serve higher-numbered ones. Plan around the resolution order.

Advanced Concepts

  1. Price wars are expensive. Cutting prices hurts your opponent but also costs you revenue. Use price reductions tactically, not as a default strategy.
  2. Roads are shared infrastructure. A road you build benefits everyone connected to it. Think about who else gains from routes you create.
  3. Use the turn order strategically. Sometimes going last is better than going first — you get to see what others do. Empty work slots help you pick your preferred turn order.
  4. Quality vs. quantity. You can sometimes earn more selling a few items at full price than selling many items at a discount. But sometimes volume wins. Read the board state.
  5. Cooperative advertising. Sometimes advertising that helps other players is fine — as long as you earn more from it than they do.

💡 Remember

The best strategy is always map-dependent. There is no single "best opening" in FCM. Read the board, watch your opponents, and adapt.
