FCM Tutorial

Common Mistakes

These are the pitfalls that trip up nearly every new player. Read through before your first game.

🔴 Critical — Almost Everyone Gets These Wrong

❌ "I have most of what they want — I should get partial credit"
✅ Demand is all-or-nothing. A house wanting 1 burger + 1 beer won't eat at your restaurant if you only have the burger. You must have EVERY item.
💡 Repeat to yourself: "All or nothing." Having 90% of what they want is the same as having 0%.
❌ "I placed a billboard, so now houses want food"
✅ Marketing creates demand in Phase 6, which happens AFTER Dinnertime (Phase 4). A campaign launched this round won't generate sales until next round.
💡 Think of it as: "You market tonight so that tomorrow people are hungry." Always plan one round ahead.
❌ "I assigned too many employees — I'll just bench the extras"
✅ If you commit more employees to your work pile than you have slots for, ALL employees (except CEO) go On the Beach. You play the entire round with just your CEO.
💡 Use the Work Planning Variant: lay cards face-down in their exact positions before committing. Count your slots carefully — managers take up a slot themselves while providing new ones.

🟠 Frequently Misunderstood

❌ "Distance counts road squares"
✅ Distance and range count tile border crossings, not road squares. Being on the same tile = distance 0. Crossing 1 tile edge = distance 1.
💡 Always say "tile borders" when counting distance. Reserve "squares" for the road grid — they're different things.
❌ "I have fewer employees, so I should go last"
✅ Players with the most empty work slots pick their turn order position first. Having fewer employees means more empty slots, which means you choose earlier.
💡 Think of it as "efficiency" — a lean company gets to choose when they act. There's a real trade-off between doing more things vs. doing them first.
❌ "I'll play this employee just for the work slots — I'll skip their action"
✅ Some employee actions are mandatory. If you play a Pricing Manager, your prices MUST go down. Drive-In signs from Local/Regional Managers MUST be placed.
💡 Rule of thumb: Phase 3 (Working 9-5) actions are mostly optional. But Drive-In signs and price changes are mandatory. Always check before you play an employee.
❌ "My entrance is just roughly where my restaurant is"
✅ Your restaurant's Entrance Corner is the specific corner marked on the token. It's the starting point for drink routes, distance calculations, campaign range — everything spatial.
💡 When placing your restaurant, choose the entrance corner carefully. It determines your entire spatial game for the rest of the match.
❌ "I'm farther away so I charge more"
✅ During competition, the house chooses based on effective price (item price + distance). But you're only paid the item price. Distance hurts your competitiveness but doesn't increase your revenue.
💡 Example: Your price is $10, distance is 2. Effective price = $12. If you win, you still only get $10 per item. Distance is a penalty, not a surcharge.
❌ "Those two roads cross, so my cart can switch between them"
✅ Bridges keep two crossing roads separate. They do NOT connect. A cart on Road A cannot switch to Road B at a bridge intersection.
💡 Think of bridges as overpasses — the roads are on different floors. They look like intersections but they're not connected.
❌ "I'll save this food for next round"
✅ ALL unsold food and drinks are discarded during Cleanup. Nothing carries over. Your stock starts empty every round.
💡 Only produce what you can sell THIS round. Match production to current demand. Overproducing is pure waste.
❌ "Each restaurant has its own kitchen inventory"
✅ All food and drinks go into a single shared stock. Any of your restaurants can sell from it. You don't assign items to specific restaurants.
💡 Think of it as a central warehouse. If you have 2 burgers, any of your restaurants can use them — but once they're sold, they're gone for all.

🟡 Subtler Gotchas

❌ "Campaign number doesn't matter — just grab any token"
✅ Campaigns resolve in numerical order. Lower-numbered campaigns go first and are more likely to successfully place demand (since houses have a 3/5 cap).
💡 Getting a low-numbered campaign token is a real strategic advantage. Don't just grab the nearest one.
❌ "I can overwrite existing demand with my own marketing"
✅ Once a house is at its demand limit (3, or 5 with garden), you cannot remove existing demand to make room. Demand stays until a restaurant serves that house.
💡 To get your demand on a house, you need to either fill empty slots or wait for someone to serve the house first, clearing its demand counters.
❌ "The garden bonus doubles each item's milestone bonus too"
✅ Gardens double the total base sale amount (item price × items sold × 2). But milestone bonuses like "+$5 per burger marketed" are NOT doubled by gardens.
💡 Garden doubles your base revenue. Milestone bonuses sit on top of that, undoubled.
