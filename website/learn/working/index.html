Phase 3: Working 9-5

This is the heart of the game — each player takes a single turn in turn order. On your turn, you perform actions A through G in order, completing all of them before the next player goes.

Most actions are optional (you can choose not to use an employee's action), but some are mandatory.

3a. Hiring

Your CEO gives you 1 free hire per round. Take any entry-level employee from the supply.

The new hire goes On the Beach — they can't work until next round. You can hire and train the same employee in the same turn if you have a Trainer.

💡 Tip

First round: You only have your CEO, so you'll just hire 1 employee. Good first hires: Marketing Trainee (you need advertising), Recruiting Girl (grow faster), or a food producer.

3b. Training

If you have a Trainer (or Coach, or Guru) in your work structure, you can upgrade employees.

  • The employee being trained must be On the Beach (not in a work slot).
  • Return the old card to the supply and take the upgraded version.
  • Check your menu card for valid training paths.
  • You can hire and train the same employee in the same turn!

🔍 Example

Hire a Management Trainee → they go On the Beach → your Trainer upgrades them into a Junior Vice President → next round, train them further into a Senior Vice President, Waitress, or Coach.

3c. Food & Drinks

Food Production

Kitchen employees produce food that goes into your shared stock. All your restaurants share one stock pool.

EmployeeProduces
Burger Cook1 burger
Burger Chef3 burgers
Pizza Cook3 pizzas
Pizza Chef8 pizzas

Drink Collection

Drinks aren't produced — they're collected from supplier locations on the map. Different employees collect drinks in different ways:

  • Errand Boy — collects 1 drink of any type, no route needed (easiest option!)
  • Cart Operator — traces a road route from your entrance, collects 2 drinks from each supplier adjacent to the route (Range 2)
  • Truck Driver — like Cart Operator but collects 3 per supplier (Range 5)
  • Zeppelin Pilot — flies by air (ignores roads), collects 2 from each supplier on tiles entered

⚠️ Watch Out

Road routes start from your Entrance Corner, not from anywhere on your restaurant. Routes trace along connected roads and range counts tile border crossings, not road squares. A Cart Operator with Range 2 can cross 2 tile borders.

⚠️ Watch Out

Stock is shared across ALL your restaurants. Food produced goes into a single pool. Any of your restaurants can sell from it — but once sold, it's gone for everyone.

More details on road routes
  • You can backtrack through previously-visited road squares, but you cannot immediately reverse direction (step back onto the square you just came from).
  • Each drink source can only be collected from once per employee per route.
  • Bridges: Two roads can cross at a bridge without connecting. A cart on Road A cannot switch to Road B at a bridge. Pretend they're on different floors.
3d. Marketing Campaigns

Use your Marketeers to place advertising campaign tokens on the map:

  1. Pick a campaign type your Marketeer can place (billboard, mailbox, airplane, or radio).
  2. Place the campaign token within the Marketeer's range from a restaurant entrance.
  3. Choose what item to advertise (burger, pizza, soda, lemonade, or beer).
  4. Choose the duration (how many rounds it will run).

⚠️ Watch Out

A busy Marketeer is LOCKED IN — they can't be used, trained, or reassigned until the campaign ends. Plan your campaign durations carefully!

⚠️ Watch Out

Campaigns don't create demand right away. They run during Phase 6 (Marketing), which happens AFTER Dinnertime. A campaign launched this round won't generate sales until next round at the earliest.

3e–g. Other Actions

Drive-Ins (Mandatory)

If you have a Local or Regional Manager in your structure, you must place Drive-In signs on all your restaurants. This gives them entrances at all 4 corners until end of round — massively expanding their reach.

Houses & Gardens

New Business Developers can place new house tokens on the map or add gardens to existing houses.

  • Houses with gardens can hold 5 demand counters (instead of 3).
  • Houses with gardens pay double price for everything they buy ($20 per item instead of $10).

Restaurants

Local Managers can open a new restaurant within road range 3 (it opens as "Coming Soon" — active next round).

Regional Managers can place a new restaurant anywhere (immediately open) or relocate an existing one.
