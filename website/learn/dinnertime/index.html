Phase 4: Dinnertime

It's Automatic

There are no decisions to make during Dinnertime. Houses are checked in numerical order (by their number on the board), and sales happen automatically based on supply, demand, and distance.

The Dinnertime Checklist

For each house (checked in numerical order), the game asks:

  1. Does this house have demand counters?
    If no → skip this house, nobody's hungry.
  2. Is there a restaurant connected by road that has ALL the items demanded?
    If no → the house stays home, no sale happens.
  3. How many restaurants can fulfill the full order?
    • Exactly one → that restaurant makes the sale.
    • Multiple → competition (lowest effective price wins, see below).
  4. Complete the sale: Remove demand counters from the house, remove items from the restaurant's stock, pay the restaurant from the bank.

All-or-Nothing

A restaurant must have ALL items the house demands to make the sale. If a house wants 1 burger + 1 drink and you only have the burger, you get nothing. No partial orders.

Payment

Each item sold earns $10. Houses with a garden pay double — $20 per item.

For example, selling 1 burger + 1 drink to a regular house earns $20. The same sale to a house with a garden earns $40.

Competition

When multiple restaurants can fulfil a house's demand, the house picks the cheapest option based on effective price:

Effective price = item price + distance (in tile border crossings)

The restaurant with the lowest effective price wins the sale. Ties are broken by: most Waitresses played → earliest in turn order.

🔍 Example

Your item price is $10, and you're 2 tiles away. Effective price = $12. Your opponent's price is also $10, but they're 0 tiles away. Effective price = $10. They win the customer. Note: distance penalizes you in competition but doesn't reduce your revenue — you'd still earn $10 per item if you won.

⚠️ Watch Out

All-or-nothing means you must stock EVERY item a house demands. If a house wants 1 burger + 1 beer and you only have the burger, they stay home. Having 90% of what they want is the same as having 0%.

⚠️ Watch Out

Effective price determines who wins the sale, but your revenue is always $10 (or $20 with garden) per item — distance doesn't reduce what you earn.

⚠️ Watch Out

Demand counters are removed after a sale. The house won't want anything again until new marketing campaigns add fresh demand. Don't count on selling to the same house twice unless you keep marketing to it.

Test Your Understanding

House #3 wants 1 burger + 1 lemonade. Restaurant A (distance 1) has 1 burger + 1 lemonade. Restaurant B (distance 0) has 2 burgers but no lemonade. Who gets the sale?

✅ Correct! Restaurant B can't fulfill the order because they don't have lemonade. It doesn't matter that they're closer — the all-or-nothing rule means only Restaurant A qualifies.

❌ Remember the all-or-nothing rule: Restaurant B doesn't have lemonade, so they can't serve this house at all. Only Restaurant A has everything the house wants.

A house WITH a garden has demand for 2 burgers. Your restaurant fulfills the order. How much do you earn?

✅ Correct! Gardens double the total payment. 2 burgers × $10 = $20, doubled to $40. Gardens are extremely valuable!

❌ Gardens double the total sale amount. The base payment is 2 × $10 = $20, and the garden doubles that to $40.
