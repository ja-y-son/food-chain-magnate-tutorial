Your Company

Your CEO

Every player starts with a CEO. Your CEO is the root of your company tree and provides:

  • 3 Work slots — spaces where employees can be placed to do their jobs each round.
  • 1 Hire action — letting you recruit one new employee from the job market each round.

The CEO never "works" like other employees. Instead, the CEO manages — providing the structure that lets your company function.

Work Slots Explained

Your company has a pyramid structure. The CEO sits at the top, and employees are placed into work slots beneath. Each employee you hire must be placed into an available slot to do their job.

Some employees also provide additional slots when placed, letting you build a deeper organization. This creates a tree: the CEO provides slots, employees fill those slots, and some of those employees provide even more slots.

On the Beach

Employees who aren't placed in work slots are "on the beach" (benched). Benched employees do nothing — they don't produce, don't market, and don't contribute to your company in any way.

However, benched employees still count toward your total headcount. In the introductory game this doesn't cost you anything (no salaries), but in the full game, every employee demands pay regardless of whether they worked.

The Overflow Catastrophe

Here's the most devastating rule in the game: during Restructuring, you assign employees face-down to your work pile. When everyone reveals, if you have more employees in your work pile than available work slots, the punishment is total — every single employee except your CEO goes On the Beach. You play the entire round with just your CEO.

You don't just lose the extras. You lose everyone.

⚠️ Watch Out

This is the #1 most punishing mistake new players make. Remember: managers occupy a slot themselves while providing slots for others. It's easy to miscount. If your CEO has 3 slots and you play a manager (taking 1 slot but providing 2 more), you have 4 remaining slots — not 5.

💡 Tip

Use the Work Planning Variant for your first game: let everyone lay cards face-down in their exact slot positions before committing. This makes it much harder to accidentally overflow.

Quick Slot-Count Check

Your CEO provides 3 work slots. You play a Management Trainee (takes 1 slot, provides 0 extra slots) and want to add 2 more employees. Can you fit them?

✅ Correct! The CEO provides 3 slots. The Management Trainee fills 1, leaving 2 for your other employees. A perfect fit.

❌ Not quite. The Management Trainee only takes up 1 regular work slot. Your CEO's 3 slots can hold the trainee plus 2 more employees.
