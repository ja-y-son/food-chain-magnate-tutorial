The Goal

You're building a fast food empire. The player with the most cash when the bank runs dry wins.

The Bank is the Clock

The game doesn't have a fixed number of rounds. Instead, the shared bank starts with $75 per player. Every time a customer pays for food, that money comes from the bank. When the bank can't fully pay for a sale, the game ends immediately. The player holding the most cash wins.

This means every sale you make drains the game's timer. Sell too aggressively and you might end the game before you've built up enough cash — or worse, you might fund your opponents' victories.

The Core Loop

HIRE people → PRODUCE food & drinks → ADVERTISE to create demand → SELL to earn cash

Each round you'll decide which employees to hire, put them to work producing goods or launching marketing campaigns, then watch as hungry customers flock to restaurants and spend money. Your job is to make sure that money flows to your register, not your opponents'.

What the Introductory Game Removes

The introductory game strips away several advanced systems so you can focus on the core experience:
