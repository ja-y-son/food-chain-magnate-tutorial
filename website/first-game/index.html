FCM Tutorial

Your First Game

A walkthrough of rounds 1–3 in a 2-player introductory game

💡 Before You Start

Use the Work Planning Variant: let everyone lay cards face-down in their exact slot positions before committing. This prevents the overflow catastrophe during your learning game.

Round 1: Just Your CEO

Phase 1 – Restructuring

You only have your CEO. No decisions to make — just flip your CEO face-up.

Phase 2 – Turn Order

Both players have 3 empty slots (CEO provides 3, none are filled). Ties are broken by previous turn order or randomly.

Phase 3 – Working 9-5

Your CEO gives you 1 free hire. This is your only action. Good first hires:

  • Marketing Trainee — you'll need advertising. Without marketing, no houses will ever have demand.
  • Errand Boy — cheapest way to start collecting drinks (1 drink, no route needed).
  • Burger Cook or Pizza Cook — start producing food early.
  • Recruiting Girl — lets you hire 2 employees next round (aggressive growth).
  • Management Trainee — the most flexible option (trains into almost anything later).

Your new hire goes On the Beach. They can't work until next round.

Phases 4–7

Dinnertime: No houses have demand yet. Nothing happens.

Marketing: No campaigns active. Nothing happens.

Cleanup: Nothing to clean up. Start round 2.

💡 Tip

Don't panic that nothing is happening. The first few rounds are about building your engine. Everyone starts slow.

Round 2: Building Your Engine

Phase 1 – Restructuring

Now you have your CEO + 1 employee from last round. Decide: put that employee to work, or keep them On the Beach?

If you hired a Marketing Trainee, put them in a work slot — you want to start a campaign.

Phase 3 – Working 9-5

You now have 2 work slots filled (or 1, depending on your hire). Key actions this round:

  • Hire another employee with your CEO's free hire.
  • Launch a campaign if you have a Marketeer — pick a food type and place a billboard near houses your restaurant can reach.
  • Produce food if you have a cook — but no one can buy it yet!

⚠️ Watch Out

If you launch a campaign now, the demand appears during Phase 6 (Marketing) — AFTER Dinnertime. No one can sell food this round. The earliest sales happen in Round 3.

Phase 6 – Marketing

If anyone launched a campaign, demand counters now appear on houses within the campaign's reach. Finally, customers know they want food!

Round 3: First Sales! 💰

The Setup

By now, some houses should have demand counters from Round 2's marketing. If you've been producing food, you might have items in stock. This is your chance to sell.

Phase 3 – Working 9-5

Your priorities this round:

  • Produce food/drinks that match the demand on nearby houses.
  • Check the all-or-nothing rule: make sure you have EVERY item a house demands, not just some of them.
  • Continue marketing to keep demand flowing for future rounds.

Phase 4 – Dinnertime

Houses are checked in numerical order. For each house:

  1. Does it have demand? ✓
  2. Is it connected to your restaurant by road? ✓
  3. Do you have ALL the items it wants? ✓
  4. If you're the only qualifying restaurant → Sale!

Collect $10 per item from the bank ($20 per item if the house has a garden). The demand counters are removed from the house.

🔍 Example

House #3 wants 1 burger. You have 1 burger in stock and your restaurant is connected by road. Sale! Collect $10 from the bank. The demand counter is removed from the house. Your burger is consumed from stock.

Key Takeaways

1. The first few rounds are quiet. Nobody can sell until houses have demand AND a restaurant has matching stock. This takes at least 2-3 rounds. Don't panic.

2. Marketing is the engine's ignition. Without marketing, no houses want food. Get a Marketeer working early.

3. Match production to demand. Only make what you can sell. Unsold food is thrown away during Cleanup.

4. Watch what your opponent does. If they're marketing burgers near houses you can also reach, produce burgers too — or market something different to avoid competition.

5. The bank is the clock. Every dollar paid to any player drains the bank. When it's getting low, make sure you've already converted your engine into cash.
