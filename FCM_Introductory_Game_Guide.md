# Food Chain Magnate — First-Time Player Guide (Introductory Game)

> *"Don't cook tonight, play Splotter's delight!"*

Welcome to **Food Chain Magnate**, a cutthroat strategy game about building a fast food empire. This guide covers everything you need to play the **Introductory Game** — a simplified version designed to teach the core mechanics without the punishing consequences of the full game.

---

## What's Different in the Introductory Game?

The introductory game **removes** several mechanics so you can focus on learning the engine:

| Full Game Rule | Introductory Game |
|---|---|
| Bank reserve cards | **Removed** — don't use them |
| Milestones | **Removed** — ignore the milestone board entirely |
| Starting bank: $50/player | **Increased** to $75/player |
| Salaries | **Removed** — skip the Payday phase completely |
| Game ends after bank breaks **twice** | Game ends after the bank breaks **once** |

**What this means for you:** You don't need to worry about paying employees, racing for milestones, or managing the two-phase bank break. Just focus on hiring people, making food, advertising, and selling.

---

## The Big Picture — How You Win

**You win by having the most cash when the bank runs out of money.**

The bank starts with a fixed amount of cash ($75 × number of players). Every time someone sells food or drinks, money flows from the bank to that player. Once the bank hits $0, the game ends immediately after the current Dinnertime phase resolves. Count your cash — most money wins.

### The Core Loop

Every round follows this cycle:

```
Build your team → Produce food & drinks → Advertise to create demand → Sell to earn cash
```

No demand on houses = no sales. No food in stock = nothing to sell. You need **both** marketing and production working together.

## Setup in 5 Minutes

### Map

| Players | Map Size | Starting Bank |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| 2 | 3×3 tiles | $150 |
| 3 | 3×4 tiles | $225 |
| 4 | 4×4 tiles | $300 |
| 5 | 4×5 tiles | $375 |

1. Lay out random map tiles in the correct grid size, randomly oriented
2. **Verify all 3 drink suppliers** (soda, lemonade, beer) are on the map — swap tiles if needed
3. Place the house+garden tokens, campaign tokens, food/drink tokens, and garden tokens near the map
4. Fill the bank with $75 per player
5. Set up the employee supply tray

### Each Player Gets

- 1 **CEO card** (pick any one)
- 1 **Menu** (your reference card)
- 3 **Restaurant tokens**
- 3 **Drive-In tokens** (Coming Soon side)
- 1 **Turn Order marker**
- **No bank reserve cards** (skip this in the introductory game)
- **No starting cash** — you begin broke!

### Place Starting Restaurants

In **reverse** turn order, each player places their first restaurant OR passes:

- Must cover 4 empty squares (no roads, no printed locations)
- The **entrance corner** (marked on the token) must be adjacent to at least 1 road
- No two starting restaurant entrances can be on the same map tile

> 💡 **Tip:** Place your restaurant entrance near drink suppliers and near areas where houses could be placed later. Connectivity is everything.

## The 6 Phases of Each Round

*(The full game has 7 phases, but the introductory game skips Payday.)*

### Phase 1: Restructuring — "Who's Working Today?"

Everyone **simultaneously** picks which employees to put to work and which to bench.

- Place employees you want to use **face-down below** your CEO
- Place employees you're benching **face-down above** your CEO (this is called "On the Beach")
- Then everyone reveals simultaneously

**Your CEO is always in play** and provides:
- **3 Work slots** (space for 3 employees underneath)
- **1 Hire action** per round

Employees must fit into available Work slots. Your CEO has 3 slots. If you play a Manager (who provides extra slots), you can fit more employees into your structure.

> ⚠️ **Critical Rule:** If you put down more employees than you have slots for, ALL your employees (except CEO) go On the Beach and you play the round with just your CEO. Use the Work Planning Variant (place cards face-down in their exact positions) to avoid this mistake!

> 💡 **First round:** You have no employees to play yet — only your CEO is available. That's normal. You'll hire on your first turn.

### Phase 2: Order of Business — "Who Goes First?"

Turn order is based on **open (empty) Work slots** — the more empty slots in your company structure, the earlier you get to pick your turn order position.

- Ties are broken by previous turn order
- Going first is often very valuable for hiring and placing campaigns

> 💡 **Why this matters:** If you play fewer employees, you have more empty slots, so you act earlier. There's a real trade-off between doing more things vs. doing them first.

### Phase 3: Working 9–5 — "Get Stuff Done"

This is where the action happens! In **turn order**, each player resolves the following sub-phases. Most actions are **optional** (you can choose not to use an employee's action).

#### 3a. Hire Employees

Your CEO gives you **1 free hire** per round. Recruiters give you more.

- Take any **entry-level employee** (ones showing the entry-level icon) from the supply
- Hired employees go **On the Beach** (you can't use them until next round)
- Check your menu card for the full employee tree

**Key entry-level employees to know:**

| Employee | What They Do |
|----------|-------------|
| **Management Trainee** | Trains into Managers, Waitresses, pricing employees, and more |
| **Recruiting Girl** | Gives you 1 extra hire action per round |
| **Marketing Trainee** | Trains into Marketeers who place advertising campaigns |
| **Errand Boy** | Collects 1 drink of any type (no route needed!) |
| **Burger Cook** | Produces 1 burger when played |
| **Pizza Cook** | Produces 3 pizzas when played |
| **Cart Operator** | Travels roads to collect 2 drinks from each supplier passed |

#### 3b. Train Employees

If you have a **Trainer**, **Coach**, or **Guru** in your structure, you can upgrade employees that are On the Beach into more powerful versions.

- The employee being trained must be **On the Beach**
- Return the old card to the supply, take the new one
- Check your menu for valid training paths
- You **can** hire and train the same employee in the same turn

> 💡 **Example:** Hire a Management Trainee → Train them into a Junior Vice President → (next round, with more train actions) train them into a Senior Vice President or CFO.

#### 3c. Open Drive-Ins *(mandatory if you have a Local/Regional Manager)*

If you played a Local or Regional Manager, you **must** place Drive-In signs on all your restaurants. This gives them entrances at all 4 corners until end of round.

#### 3d. Launch Marketing Campaigns

Use your played **Marketeers** to place advertising tokens on the map.

1. Pick a campaign type your Marketeer can place (billboard, mailbox, airplane, or radio)
2. Place the token within the Marketeer's range from a restaurant entrance
3. Choose what item to advertise and how many rounds it will run
4. The Marketeer becomes **busy** — they can't be used until the campaign ends

> ⚠️ **Critical:** A busy Marketeer is locked in. You can't use them, train them, or (in the full game) fire them while they're running a campaign.

> 💡 **Remember:** Campaigns don't create demand right away. They run during Phase 6 (Marketing), which happens AFTER Dinnertime. So a campaign launched this round won't generate sales until **next round at the earliest**.

#### 3e. Prep Food & Drinks

Produce food and collect drinks to build up your **stock**.

**Food (Kitchen Employees):**
- Burger Cook → 1 burger
- Pizza Cook → 3 pizzas
- Higher-level cooks produce more

**Drinks (Drink Collectors):**
- **Errand Boy** → 1 drink of any type (no route needed — easiest to use!)
- **Cart Operator** → Traces a road route from your restaurant entrance, collects 2 drinks from each supplier adjacent to the route
- **Truck Driver** → Same as Cart Operator but collects 3 from each supplier
- **Zeppelin Pilot** → Flies by air, collects 2 drinks from each supplier on tiles entered

> 💡 **Key concept — Road Routes:** Cart Operators and Truck Drivers must trace a connected road path from your restaurant's entrance corner. They can only collect from suppliers that are **orthogonally adjacent** to their route path. Range = max number of tile borders crossed.

#### 3f. Place Houses & Gardens

**New Business Developers** can place new house+garden tokens on the map or add gardens to existing houses. Houses are where your customers live!

- Houses with gardens can hold **5 demand counters** (instead of 3)
- Houses with gardens pay **double price** for everything they buy

#### 3g. Place/Move Restaurants

**Local Managers** can open a new restaurant within road range 3 (it opens as "Coming Soon" and becomes active next round).

**Regional Managers** can place a new restaurant **anywhere** (immediately open) or relocate an existing one.

### Phase 4: Dinnertime — "Show Me the Money!" 💰

This is where you get paid. **No decisions are made** — it's all automatic.

Houses are checked **in numerical order** (lowest number first). For each house:

1. Does it have any **demand counters**? If no → stays home
2. Is it **connected by road** to any open restaurant? If no → stays home
3. Does any connected restaurant have **all the items** matching the house's demand in stock? If no → stays home
4. If exactly one restaurant qualifies → the house eats there
5. If multiple restaurants qualify → **competition** (see below)

**Selling:**
- Discard the demand counters from the house
- Discard matching items from your stock
- Collect **$10 per item sold** from the bank (base item price)
- Houses with **gardens pay double** ($20 per item!)

> ⚠️ **All-or-nothing rule:** You must be able to fulfill **every** demand counter on a house. If a house wants 1 burger + 1 beer and you only have the burger, they stay home. Customers are picky!

> ⚠️ **Demand counters are removed** after a sale. The house won't want anything again until new marketing campaigns add fresh demand.

#### Competition (Multiple Restaurants Can Serve a House)

When multiple restaurants compete for the same house:

1. Calculate each restaurant's **effective price** = item price + road distance (each tile = $1)
2. Lowest effective price wins
3. Ties broken by: most **Waitresses** played → earliest in **turn order**

> 💡 **Example:** Your item price is $10 and you're 2 tiles away. Effective price = $12. Your opponent's item price is $10 but they're 0 tiles away. Effective price = $10. They win the customer.

### ~~Phase 5: Payday~~ — **SKIPPED in Introductory Game**

No salaries to pay! Hire freely without worrying about upkeep costs.

### Phase 6: Marketing — "Campaigns Generate Demand"

Campaigns run **automatically** in numerical order:

1. Each active campaign places **1 demand counter** of its type on every house within its reach
2. Remove 1 duration counter from the campaign
3. If no duration counters remain, the campaign ends and the Marketeer goes On the Beach

**Campaign reach:**
- **Billboard** → reaches houses orthogonally adjacent to the token
- **Mailbox** → reaches houses in line-of-sight (blocked only by roads and map edges)
- **Airplane** → reaches houses in its flyover zone (1, 3, or 5 rows wide)
- **Radio tower** → reaches every house on its tile and all 8 surrounding tiles

> 💡 **Houses max out at 3 demand counters** (5 with a garden). Extra marketing on a full house does nothing.

### Phase 7: Cleanup — "Reset for Next Round"

1. **Throw away** all unsold food/drinks in your stock (back to supply)
2. **Return** all employees to your hand (busy Marketeers stay out)
3. **Remove** all Drive-In and Coming Soon signs (restaurants are now open)
4. Start the next round!

> ⚠️ **You lose unsold stock every round.** Don't overproduce — only make what you can sell.

## The Employee Tree — What to Hire and Train

Your **menu card** shows all training paths. Here's the essential overview:

### Food Production Path
```
Burger Cook → Burger Chef (3 burgers) → (nothing further)
Pizza Cook  → Pizza Chef (8 pizzas)  → (nothing further)
```

### Drink Collection Path
```
Errand Boy → Cart Operator → Truck Driver → Zeppelin Pilot
```

### Marketing Path
```
Marketing Trainee → Campaign Manager → Brand Manager → Brand Director
```
Each level unlocks more campaign types, longer durations, and greater range.

### Management Path (most versatile)
```
Management Trainee → Junior VP → Senior VP → many options:
                                              → Waitress / CFO
                                              → Recruiting Manager / HR Director
                                              → Coach / Guru
                                              → Pricing Manager / Discount Manager / Luxuries Manager
                                              → New Business Developer
                                              → Local Manager / Regional Manager
```

> 💡 **For your first game:** Focus on a simple engine. You probably want some combination of: a cook, a drink collector, and a marketeer. Then expand from there.

## Key Concepts to Remember

### Distance & Range

- Distance is counted by **tile border crossings**, not squares
- Being on the same tile as a location = distance 0
- **Road range:** trace along connected roads
- **Air range:** count orthogonally adjacent tiles (ignore roads)
- Your employee's range number = max tile borders they can cross

### The Map

- **Entrance Corner** is everything — it's the only point your restaurant connects to roads from
- Two locations are "connected by road" if they both touch the same continuous road
- **Bridges** keep two crossing roads separate — they don't connect at the intersection

### Stock is Shared

All your restaurants share a single stock. If you have 2 burgers, any of your restaurants can sell them. But once they're sold, they're gone for all restaurants.

### Information is (Mostly) Public

- Everyone's cash is public
- Everyone's employees are public (except during the Restructuring face-down step)
- Everyone's stock is public
- The only secret: which employees you're choosing to play vs. bench each round

## First Game Strategy Tips

### Round 1: You Can Only Hire

You start with just a CEO. Use your 1 hire action. Good first hires:

- **Marketing Trainee** — you'll need advertising eventually
- **Errand Boy** — cheapest way to start collecting drinks
- **Burger Cook or Pizza Cook** — start producing food early
- **Recruiting Girl** — if you want to hire 2 employees next round
- **Management Trainee** — the most flexible option (trains into almost anything)

### The First Few Rounds Are Quiet

Nobody can sell anything until houses have demand counters. Demand only appears after a Marketeer launches a campaign AND the Marketing phase runs. This means:

- **Round 1:** Hire your first employee
- **Round 2:** Play your Marketeer, launch a campaign. At end of round (Marketing phase), demand appears on houses
- **Round 3 (earliest):** If you have food/drinks in stock, you can finally sell at Dinnertime!

Use the early rounds to build your engine. Don't panic that nothing is happening.

### Don't Overproduce

You **throw away** unsold items at end of round. Only produce what you can actually sell. Match your production to the demand on houses your restaurant can reach.

### Location, Location, Location

Your restaurant's **entrance corner** determines what you can reach. Think about:
- Which houses are connected to your entrance?
- Which drink suppliers can your collectors reach?
- Where should you place campaigns for maximum effect?

### Watch What Others Are Doing

- If an opponent is marketing burgers near houses you can also reach, prepare burgers too — or market something different
- If you both can fulfill the same house's demand, **distance** and **waitresses** determine who gets the sale
- You can place campaigns that create demand for items your opponents can't produce

### The Bank Is the Clock

Every dollar earned by any player drains the bank. If everyone is selling a lot, the game ends fast. If nobody is selling, the game goes long. Watch the bank — when it's getting low, make sure you've already converted your engine into cash.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Forgetting that demand comes AFTER Dinnertime** — your campaign runs in Phase 6, but sales happen in Phase 4. New demand won't help you until next round.

2. **Not having all items a house wants** — if a house has demand for 1 burger + 1 beer, you need BOTH. Having just one means no sale at all.

3. **Ignoring road connections** — your restaurant must be connected to a house **by road** for that house to eat there. Adjacency alone isn't enough if there's no road connection.

4. **Overproducing** — unsold stock is thrown away every round. Produce only what you expect to sell.

5. **Forgetting your entrance corner** — all distances and routes start from your restaurant's entrance corner, not from the restaurant in general.

6. **Playing too many employees and overflowing your work slots** — if your employees don't fit in your available slots, ALL of them go On the Beach. Use the Work Planning Variant!

7. **Not checking house numbers** — houses are served in numerical order. If you only have enough stock for one house, the lower-numbered house gets served first.
