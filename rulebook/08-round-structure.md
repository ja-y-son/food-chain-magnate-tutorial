# Round Structure

The game is played in rounds until the ending condition is met. Each round is broken up into **7 phases**:

| Phase | Name |
|---|---|
| 1 | Restructuring |
| 2 | Order of Business |
| 3 | Working 9–5 |
| 4 | Dinnertime |
| 5 | Payday |
| 6 | Marketing |
| 7 | Cleanup |

## Phase 1: Restructuring

Simultaneously, all players select employees from their hand to play, then the remaining employees go On the beach. Do this as follows:

1. Select employees from your hand to play this round, placing them in a face-down stack below your CEO.
2. Place the remaining employees from your hand into a face-down stack above your CEO (On the beach).

> **Note:** Ignore selecting employees to play during the first round of the game — you have none to play (only your CEO is available and already automatically played each round).

### Work Planning Variant

In the normal game, you play stacks of cards, so the other players do not know how many cards you plan to play versus send On the beach. For new players, or those looking for a more casual experience, you can plan out your structure more easily by placing your cards face down in the exact spots they will go below your CEO and Managers — this helps with planning your available space!

### Employees On the Beach

- You may Train them.
- You must pay their salary.
- They provide no actions or Work slots for you this round.
- On the beach employees are placed face-up so all players can see them.

## Company Structure and CEO Slots

Your company structure is the arrangement of employee cards played below your CEO. Employees must be placed in available **Work slots** — spaces provided by your CEO and by Manager-type employees.

### CEO Slots

Your CEO starts with a number of Work slots. If your CEO has 3 slots, you can have at most 3 additional Work-slot-bearing employee cards working at the same time.

All played employees must be assigned to Work slots. If you are unable to assign all of them, you must place **all** of your employees (except for your CEO) in your face-up On the beach pile, and play the round with just your CEO. Due to the challenging nature of this, even experienced players may opt to use the Work Planning Variant.

> **Note:** You may play any number of employees to allow for open (unoccupied) Work slots — doing this impacts turn order (Order of Business phase). In short, more empty spots help!

### Viewing Opponents' Employees

Employees owned are always public information except while in hand and playing them to Work slots or On the beach in face-down stacks. Once stacks are revealed, all employees are viewable again by anyone.

### Mandatory Employee Actions

Most employee actions you use throughout the round are optional, but the following actions are considered **mandatory**. This means that if you play an employee with this action in your company structure, you are committed to using that action later in the round, even if it is no longer helpful to you:

- Placing "Drive-In" signs (Local Manager, Regional Manager)
- Lowering or raising your item price (Pricing Manager, Luxuries Manager, Discount Manager)
