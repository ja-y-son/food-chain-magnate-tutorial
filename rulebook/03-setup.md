# Setup

## Player Count Reference

| | 2 Players | 3 Players | 4 Players | 5 Players |
|---|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Map Size** | 3×3 tiles | 3×4 tiles | 4×4 tiles | 4×5 tiles |
| **Starting Bank** | $100 | $150 | $200 | $250 |
| **1× Employees Used** | 1 of each type | 1 of each type | 2 of each type | All |
| **Billboards Removed** | #12, #15, #16 | #15, #16 | #16 | None |

## Map & Supply Setup

1. **MAP:** Lay out random map tiles in a grid of the size and dimensions listed for your player count. Orient each tile at random. Return all unused tiles to the box.

   > **Note:** Don't worry about roads that do not connect to anything.

2. **HOUSE SUPPLY:** Place the 8 houses with attached gardens in a supply area near the map.

3. **CAMPAIGN SUPPLY:** Place the marketing campaign tokens and busy tokens in the supply. With fewer than 5 players, remove the billboard campaigns and matching busy tokens listed for your player count from the game.

4. **GENERAL SUPPLY:** Place all 8 separate garden tokens, all food and drink tokens, and 1 freezer tile per player in the supply. Return unused freezer tiles to the box.

5. **BANK:** Fill the bank with $50 cash per player. Leave the remaining cash in the box.

   > **Note:** The leftover cash will be used later in the game (see pg. 28).

6. **UNIQUE EMPLOYEES:** Find the 10 employee types marked with a **1×**. With fewer than 5 players, remove cards of each 1× employee type from the game until you have the number listed for your player count of each type (e.g., with 2 players, remove 2 of each 1× employee so you have 1 left of each, or 10 cards in total).

7. **EMPLOYEE SUPPLY:** Slot the selected 1× employee cards in the employee supply tray. For each other employee type (without a 1×), slot all available cards in the tray, regardless of player count.

## Player Setup

1. **PLAYER PIECES:** Each player picks a fast food chain and gathers the following components:

   > **Note:** Players do not start with any cash.

   - a. Menu
   - b. Milestone tray
   - c. CEO card (select any 1)
   - d. Turn Order marker
   - e. 3 Restaurant tokens
   - f. 3 Drive-In tokens
   - g. 3 Bank Reserve cards (1 each of $100, $200, $300)

2. **CEO:** Each player places their CEO card in front of themselves.

3. **MILESTONES:** The base-game milestones are printed on the board itself.

   > **Note:** If playing with expansion milestones, use the expansion insert.

4. **TURN ORDER:** Place the turn order track near the map and line up players' turn order markers in a random starting order.

5. **BANK RESERVE:** Each player secretly chooses 1 bank reserve card to place facedown near the bank (return the other 2 cards hidden to the game box). See *Choosing Bank Reserve Cards* on page 6.

6. **STARTING RESTAURANTS:** Starting with the last player on the turn order track and going in reverse turn order, each player takes one turn to either: place their starting restaurant in a legal location; or pass (see below).

   - All players that passed (if any) now place their starting restaurant, but now proceed in turn order.
   - *For example, in a 3-player game, the 3rd player in turn order places first, but then the other 2 players each decide to pass. Next, the 1st player in turn order places, then finally the 2nd player.*

### Starting Restaurant Placement Rules

You can place your starting Restaurant anywhere on the map following these rules:

- It must cover 4 empty squares (it cannot cover roads, printed locations, or other restaurants); **and**
- Its Entrance Corner must be placed adjacent to 1 or more roads; **and**
- Its Entrance Corner must not be on the same tile as another restaurant's Entrance Corner.

> **Note:** Any other part of the restaurant may be on the same tile as another restaurant.

## Introductory Game

Food Chain Magnate is a very unforgiving game. If you make a mistake, it can be very hard or even impossible to get back on track. For your first game, we strongly recommend you modify the rules as follows:

- Do not use bank reserve cards.
- Do not use milestones.
- Ensure that all 3 drink supplier types are on the map.
- Start with $75 per player in the bank (instead of $50).
- Do not pay any salaries (skip the Payday phase each round).
- End the game the first time the bank breaks.

This will make for a somewhat shorter game where everyone can get to know the rules without running their companies into the ground. For your second game, we recommend making these same modifications, but adding in milestones.

> **Note:** Some milestones do not provide useful benefits in an introductory game.
