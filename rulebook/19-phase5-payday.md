# Phase 5: Payday

During this phase, players simultaneously choose whether to fire any of their employees. Each player must then pay for each of their employees who requires a salary.

## Firing Employees

Before you pay salaries, you may fire any number of employees played in your company structure or On the Beach. Return each employee you fire to the supply. You **cannot** voluntarily fire busy Marketeers (see sidebar below for details).

## Paying Salaries

You must pay a **$5 salary** to the bank for each of your employees that shows a salary icon. This includes employees played in your company structure, On the Beach, or busy running marketing campaigns (unless you have the "First Billboard Campaign" milestone — they have no salary in this case).

If you do not have enough cash to pay, you must fire salaried employees to lower the amount you owe until you can pay the full remaining amount. In the extreme case that you have $0 in cash, you must fire all of your salaried employees.

## Salary Discounts

If you receive any salary discounts from milestones or played employees in your company structure, you must apply them to the total amount you owe before paying anything.

You may receive salary discounts from the following sources:

- **−$5** per unused Recruiting Manager action played in your company structure
- **−$5** per unused HR Director action played in your company structure
- **−$15** if you have the "First to Train an Employee" milestone

All salary discounts are **mandatory**, meaning you must use each salary discount available to you, even if it is not helpful to you. (Specifically, you cannot choose to ignore a discount in order to earn the "First to Pay $20 or More in Salaries" milestone.) No matter how many salary discounts you have, you cannot reduce the total you owe below $0 — employees will not pay to work for you!

## Paying and Firing Busy Marketeers

While your Marketeers are busy running campaigns, you must still pay their salaries (unless you have the "First Billboard Placed" milestone — they have no salary in this case). You can **never** voluntarily fire a busy Marketeer.

In the rare case that you cannot pay a busy Marketeer's salary and you have no other salaried employees you can fire instead, you must fire the busy Marketeer. Their campaign remains on the map and continues running normally until it runs out of counters.

## Payday Milestones

If you pay a total amount of **$20 or more** to the bank during the Payday phase while the "First to Pay $20 or More in Salaries" milestone is available, immediately claim it and receive its benefit. You must actually pay this amount to the bank; if the salary discounts you receive reduce the final amount you pay below $20, you cannot claim the milestone.
