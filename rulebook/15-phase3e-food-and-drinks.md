# Phase 3e: Prep Food & Drinks

## General Rules

When you Produce or Collect food or drink, take them from the supply and put them in front of you (in your stock). Gaining food and drink is **optional**.

Employees gain food and drink differently as follows:

## Kitchen Employees (Producing Food)

Gain a number of food counters as stated on each of your played Kitchen employees.

> **Example:** When you play a Pizza Cook, you gain 3 pizza.

## Collecting Drinks

Gain drinks for each of your played drink collectors as follows:

- **Errand Boys** allow you to gain any 1 drink (no restrictions — the supplier doesn't even need to be on the map at all!).
- **Cart Operators and Truck Drivers** must follow a Road Route to collect drinks. You must collect from each supplier (soda, lemonade, and beer location on the map) that is orthogonally adjacent to your traced route path.
  - Cart Operators gain **2 drinks** from each orthogonally adjacent supplier they pass.
  - Truck Drivers gain **3 drinks** from each instead.
- **Zeppelin Pilots** must gain 2 drinks from each supplier showing on each map tile their route enters (including the starting tile which contains the Entrance Corner). A Zeppelin Pilot cannot visit a map tile more than once.

In all cases, each source can only be collected from **once** (per employee, per round), regardless of the number of times you pass by it on each route.

> **Clarification:** Each separate drink collector you have can collect from the same supplier during the same turn.

## Trace a Road Route

**Start:** A route must start on an Entrance Corner of one of your open restaurants (or a coffee shop from the expansion).

**Road Route:** You can only trace along connected road section "squares". (To clarify, road movement is done in square-by-square movement; consider all roads to be broken up into a grid as the rest of the map tile.) All edges of orthogonally-adjacent road segment "squares" are considered connected, and can be traced along (regardless of how the art might look; i.e., the yellow road line does not prevent road connections).

## Range

The Range of an employee is shown at the top-right corner of the card. Road Range and Air Range function the same in this regard: this number tells you the **maximum number of times your traced route can cross a tile border**.

> **Range Example:** Xango must have Range 2 to reach house #2, or Range 3 to reach house #1. House #1 and Xango are on the same tile, but are separated by 3 map-tile "borders". If Xango had a drive-in (via Local or Regional Manager), "0 Range" would be enough to reach house #1.

## Backtracking

The route may trace back along a road section "square" that was previously traced along this route (or a map tile can be entered more than once) with the following clarifications:

- You **cannot** trace a road route path back onto a road segment square that you immediately-previously traced from.

> **Example:** A road route could not trace to collect the lemonade, then move back onto a road section square it just came from.

## Collection Example

To collect both the soda and lemonade, Range 3 is required (e.g., Truck Driver, or Cart Operator with milestone). 3 Range is traced with this road path, as it crosses the border of a map tile 3 times.

- The lemonade is **not** collected from the start of the route even though it is orthogonally adjacent to the restaurant's entrance.
- The lemonade could not be collected first, as it would require you to trace a road route path back onto a road segment square that you just traced from.
- The soda is only collected **once**, even though it is passed by an orthogonally-adjacent route 3 times.

To clarify, if your Entrance Corner is at the edge of a map tile, it requires **1 Range** to get onto the road that is on the adjacent tile, but your traced path does NOT begin until you are on that road.

> **Example:** From Gluttony, it takes 1 Range just to gain lemonade from the shown supplier, even though it is directly adjacent to the Entrance Corner that you start tracing your path from. If you decide to trace your route down, towards house 2, you will not gain the Lemonade.

## Road Distance and Multiple Connected Roads

When an Entrance Corner is adjacent to 2 separate roads, when calculating distance using road routes, choose **one road** to travel along for each road-traveling employee.

> **Example:** If you have a single Truck Driver, you can choose to get lemonade on Road A, or soda on Road B, but not both, because the roads do not connect together!

> **Clarification:** If you have 2 separate Truck Drivers, you could send one of them to get the soda, and the other to get the lemonade; you just cannot send the same road-traveling employee to both of them. Road A does not connect to Road B through the restaurant Entrance Corner.
