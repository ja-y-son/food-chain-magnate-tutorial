# Item Counters

Food counters (burgers and pizzas) and drink counters (soda, lemonade, and beer) are collectively referred to as **item counters**. These are used to track a variety of things during the game. The number of item counters in the game is not intended to be limited; if you run out of any item, use a suitable proxy.

> **Note:** Demand and duration are both tracked by item counters.

## Uses of Item Counters

- **Stock:** Items in front of you represent the food and drinks your restaurants have available to sell at Dinnertime.
- **Demand:** Counters on a house show what the customers who live there want to buy. A house can have up to 3 demand counters on it at a time, or 5 if it has a garden.
- **Duration:** Counters on a marketing campaign show what item it is creating demand for and how many more rounds it has left to run.

# Milestones

Milestones are rewards you receive for achieving certain goals during the game. Each milestone provides an immediate or ongoing benefit that can change the course of the game. It is vital to plan your strategy around which milestones you can claim!

## Claiming Milestones

When you fulfill the requirements for an available milestone, place a checkmark token covering it on your milestone tray and announce that you have claimed it.

If you fulfill an available milestone's requirements in the same round as one or more of your opponents, **each of you claims the milestone** and receives its full benefits.

At the end of the round in which a milestone is claimed, each player who did **not** claim the milestone must place an ✗ token covering it to indicate it is no longer available.

## Mandatory Benefits

All benefits of each milestone are **mandatory**, meaning you must apply them even when they are not helpful to you. For example, if you have the "First Billboard Campaign" milestone, then all future marketing campaigns you launch must be eternal, even if you would rather some of them be temporary.

> **Example:** Golden Duck played a Trainer this round and Trained one of their employees. No other player has done this in this game so far, so Golden Duck places a checkmark on the "First to Train an Employee" milestone to show they have a $15 salary discount for the rest of the game. At the end of the round, every other player places an ✗ on their boards on "First to Train an Employee" to show that they cannot gain this milestone anymore during this game.
