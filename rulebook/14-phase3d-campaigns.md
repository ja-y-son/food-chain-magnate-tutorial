# Phase 3d: Launch Campaigns

You may use each of your played Marketeers in Work slots to launch **1 new marketing campaign** for a food or drink item of your choice.

Each Marketeer card shows the following:

- **A.** Type of campaign they can place
- **B.** Range they can travel from a restaurant to place their campaign
- **C.** Maximum number of rounds the campaign can run for

> **Example:** A Marketing Trainee can only place a billboard campaign, which runs for up to 2 rounds, within a Range of 2 by road of a restaurant. In comparison, a Brand Director can place a radio, airplane, mailbox, or billboard campaign, which runs for up to 5 rounds, in any legal location on the map.

## Launching a Campaign

To launch a new campaign, choose a Marketeer in your company structure and follow these steps:

### Step 1: Choose Campaign

Take any currently available campaign token from the supply of a type indicated by your played Marketeer.

### Step 2: Place Campaign

Place the campaign token within the Marketeer's Range, starting from any of your open restaurant's Entrance Corners.

- **Billboard, radio, and mailbox** campaign tokens must be placed on empty squares and connected to a road.
  - If the Marketeer used travels by road, the token must be connected to the road they used.
- **Airplane** campaign tokens ignore roads. These tokens must be placed adjacent to the outer edge of the map, aligned with any outer map tile(s), but they have a specific orientation that must be followed:
  - The edge of the airplane token that is size 1, 3, or 5 squares long must be the side that is aligned with the edge of the map. It must align with exactly that number of map squares (i.e., an airplane cannot "stick out" beyond any corner of the map).
  - Airplane tokens may **not** overlap other airplane tokens.
  - Airplanes must always be adjacent to a map tile; however, their paths may overlap when across from each other on the outer edges of the map. This may result in multiple airplanes marketing to the same house(s).

### Step 3: Choose Item & Duration

Choose any **1 food or drink item** to advertise. Take item counters of the chosen type from the supply and place them on the campaign as duration counters. Place 1 duration counter per round you want the campaign to last, up to the maximum duration listed on the Marketeer's card.

> **Strategy Tip:** You might have a campaign run fewer rounds than its maximum so the Marketeer card becomes available again sooner for a different campaign, or to avoid creating more demand than your restaurants can handle.

### Step 4: Place Busy Marker

Remove the Marketeer from your company structure and place them face up above your CEO, beside your On the Beach pile. Find the busy marker with the same number as the campaign and place it on the Marketeer's card to show which campaign they are running. The Marketeer remains busy until the campaign ends.

## Busy Marketeers, Items, and Duration

Once a Marketeer launches a campaign, they must remain busy running the campaign until it ends (i.e., you cannot end it early). While a Marketeer is busy, they are **neither** considered "played" in a Work slot **nor** "On the Beach". Once a campaign is running, you cannot change its duration or prematurely cancel it. A campaign must continue running until its duration counters run out, even if it is no longer helpful to the player who launched it.

- You **cannot** play a busy Marketeer, use their action, or Train them.
- You **must** pay their salary, and you **cannot** fire them (unless you are broke).

## Campaign Reach

### Billboard and Mailbox Campaign Reach

The size of the token determines the area it reaches (except for mailboxes). Billboards reach all houses adjacent to them — a larger billboard can create demand on more houses.

An airplane with a 5-row flyover zone will generally reach more houses than an airplane with a 1-row flyover zone.

The reach of a **mailbox** campaign is not affected by its size, but a smaller mailbox is sometimes easier to place on the map.

All **radio** campaigns are the same size.

### Adding Demand Order / Campaign Number

Campaigns add demand to houses in order from **lowest to highest** number during the Marketing phase.

> **Strategy Tip:** Generally, a campaign that runs earlier is more likely to successfully place demand than a campaign that runs later, because once a house reaches the limit of 3 demand counters (or 5 with a garden), no more demand can be added to it.

> **Strategy Tip:** It is allowed to place a marketing campaign in a location where it will not reach any houses. This could be a strategic move to block part of the map, or it could be in preparation for adding houses in later rounds.

## Eternal Campaigns

If you have the **"First Billboard Placed"** milestone, all new marketing campaigns you run must be **eternal**, meaning they have unlimited duration and are never removed from the map.

After gaining the "First Billboard Placed" milestone, you must flip all new campaign tokens to their "eternal" side (showing an infinity sign), and you only place **1 duration counter** on each of them from now on (do this now with the billboard you just placed to gain this milestone). This counter is not removed during the Marketing phase. A Marketeer running an eternal campaign remains busy for the rest of the game, and can never again be played, trained, or fired (because they no longer have a salary).
