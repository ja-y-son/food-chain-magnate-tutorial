# Game Overview

In Food Chain Magnate, play as a lone entrepreneur attempting to build your company into a thriving restaurant empire. You do this by hiring and training employees, running marketing campaigns to generate demand, and serving food and drink to paying customers.

## Object of the Game

Your goal is to have the **most total cash** at the end of the game. The game ends after the bank breaks (i.e., runs out of cash) **twice**.

- The **first** time the bank breaks, players' chosen bank reserve cards are revealed, and the bank is refilled with the total amount of cash shown on them.
- The **second** time the bank breaks, players finish collecting their earnings for the round, and then the game ends. Whoever has the most cash wins (see pg. 28 and pg. 33 for details).

> The amount of cash each player has is always **public information**.

## Choosing Bank Reserve Cards

The bank reserve cards you and your opponents choose at setup determine how long the game will last — and also how many CEO slots each player will have after the first bank break!

- Playing a **high-value** card increases the total amount of cash that can be earned before triggering the end of the game.
- Playing a **low-value** card reduces it.

Longer or shorter games favor different strategies, but be aware that you will not initially know whether your opponents chose to shorten or lengthen the game, so **flexibility can be important**.

> **Note:** First-time players will likely not be familiar with long- or short-term strategies, so we recommend playing without bank reserve cards in an introductory game (see pg. 5).

## Game Concepts

### Employee Cards

Employee cards represent the people who work for your restaurant. You start the game with only a CEO, but you can hire cooks, waitresses, marketeers, managers, and others to perform various restaurant tasks.

At the start of each round, each player simultaneously chooses employees from their hand to play into Work slots, then your remaining un-played employees go "On the beach" (in a stack above your CEO card, see pg. 12). Your played employees determine the actions available to you during the round.
