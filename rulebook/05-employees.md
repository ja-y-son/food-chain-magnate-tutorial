# Employees

## Employee Cards

Employee cards represent the people who work for your restaurant. You start the game with only a CEO, but you can hire cooks, waitresses, marketeers, managers, and others to perform various restaurant tasks.

At the start of each round, each player simultaneously chooses employees from their hand to play into Work slots, then your remaining un-played employees go "On the beach" (in a stack above your CEO card). Your played employees determine the actions available to you during the round.

## Card Anatomy

- **A. Entry-Level Employee:** When the entry-level icon is showing at the top-left corner of an Employee card, you can directly Hire them.
- **B. Trained Employee:** Employees showing no icon at their top-left corner must be Trained from their corresponding employee.
- **C. Action:** Describes what this employee does when played to work in your company structure.
- **D. Range:** Gives the maximum distance this employee can travel from your restaurant to take an action on the map.
  1. A **road range** means they travel on connected roads only.
  2. An **air range** means they travel by air, ignoring roads.
- **E. Training Options:** Lists the higher-level employee types that you can train this employee into. If none are listed, the employee can't be trained.
- **F. Work Slots:** Shows how many employees you can play below the card in your company structure.
- **G. Unique Employee:** You can only have 1 employee of this type in your company.
- **H. Salary:** You must pay $5 each round if you have this employee (in a Work slot or On the beach).

## Employee Types

### Managers

Managers provide slots that expand your company structure and allow you to play more employees each round. They can also be trained into a variety of other employee types.

### Waitresses, New Business Developers, and CFOs

Waitresses and CFOs provide extra cash at the end of Dinnertime. New business developers place new customer houses and gardens on the map.

### Planning Employees

Local and Regional Managers allow you to place new restaurants or move existing ones on the map. They also add drive-ins to your restaurants, which temporarily give them entrances at all 4 corners.

### Pricing Employees

Pricing employees temporarily lower or raise the item price you charge customers. Lowering your price helps you compete for more customers; raising it makes you more money off each sale.

### Recruiters & Trainers

Recruiters hire new entry-level employees. Trainers train the employees you've hired into higher-level types.

### Marketeers

Marketeers place marketing campaigns on the map and run them for 1 or more rounds. While a marketeer is running a campaign, they are "busy" and can't be played or trained.

### Drink Buyers

Drink buyers collect drinks to sell to customers at Dinnertime. Except for Errand Boys, each buyer travels a drink route on the map to collect from drink supplier locations.

### Kitchen Employees

Kitchen employees produce burgers and pizzas to sell to customers at Dinnertime.
